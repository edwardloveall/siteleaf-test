Whoever you are, you are welcome!

Unitarian Universalism celebrates the inherent worth and dignity of all people. First Parish is truly defined by our mission statement.

We choose to be a liberal religious community, welcoming to all. We encourage each other on our spiritual journeys, support one another through the changes in our lives, and challenge the excesses and injustices of our time. Called to love and upheld by joy, we live our faith.

The over 400 members of First Parish gather from many different walks of life. We build community and friendships, single or coupled, across race, ethnicity, gender-identity/expression, sexual orientation, income, occupation, age, ability, and belief(s)/non-belief.

Our vibrant religious education program, including an active youth group and children's program, is the largest Unitarian Universalist religious education program in New England and we are consistently humbled and amazed by our youths' accomplishments.

First Parish members are incredibly dedicated to social justice causes. Among our social justice efforts:

  • we support and house asylum seekers
  • we work for legislation combating mass incarceration
  • we challenge our unconscious biases and fight against institutional racism both within our congregation and outside our walls
  • we value sustainable living, are proud of our solar panels
  • we support accessibility in our building

Music is a powerful part of our Sunday services. Our congregation enjoys multiple vocal choirs, a woodwind ensemble, a children's choir and a bell choir along with solo performers of every instrument. The music is performed by the talented, multi-generational, musicians in our congregation, providing diversity and bringing us together.

Located in the center of Arlington, we have been around since the founding of the town. Through the generations we have stood for progressive reform. Learn more about First Parish's long, rich history.

Meeting Space for People of Color

Because of the continuing injustice and violence disproportionately faced by people of color, we affirm that Black Lives Matter.

In keeping with Black Lives UU best practices, First Parish offers free meeting space for organizations run by people of color such as Black Lives Matter, CAIRS, etc.

Visit here to inquire.

What Does it Mean to be UU?

We are people of all ages, people of many backgrounds, and people of many beliefs. We are brave, curious and compassionate thinkers and doers. We create spirituality and community beyond boundaries, working for more justice and more love in our own lives and in the world.

 

Unitarian Universalism affirms and promotes seven Principles, grounded in the humanistic teachings of the world's religions. Our spirituality is unbounded, drawing from scripture and science, nature and philosophy, personal experience and ancient tradition as described in our six Sources.

We are a Welcoming Congregation
